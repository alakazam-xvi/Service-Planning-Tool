About the Simulator

Overview

This simulator provides a deterministic model for transit planning, designed to visualize how a fleet of vehicles handles a schedule across varying operational periods in a day. It is specifically tailored to discretize variations in Roundtrip (RT) times resulting from changing traffic conditions (e.g., peak vs. off-peak hours).

Processes & Formulas

  • Total Operating Time: Computed as the sum of all individual Period Durations. Any trip started must be completed before this total time elapses.
  • Dispatch Interval (Headway): Calculated per period as (Roundtrip Time + Layover) / Number of Units. Cycle time includes both travel and terminal recovery time, giving a realistic headway.
  • Trip Generation Algorithm:
    • Unit 1 departs at Start of Operation. Subsequent units are staggered by the Period 1 Dispatch Interval.
    • For any given departure time, the simulator determines which "Period" the time falls into to assign the correct Roundtrip Time.
    • The next departure for a unit is calculated as Current Start Time + Current Roundtrip Time + Layover.

Key Assumptions

  • Homogeneous Fleet: All units are identical and operate at the assumed speeds of their respective periods.
  • Explicit Layover: Recovery time, terminal dwell, and driver buffer are modeled as a separate global input. Roundtrip Time should represent travel time only; layover is added on top to compute cycle time and headway.
  • Punctuality: Vehicles depart exactly as scheduled, without probabilistic traffic delays or stochastic bunching.

Limitations (Transport Planning Context)

  • Non-Loop Routes: True non-loop routes require modeling A-to-B and B-to-A deadheading or dwell times at terminals. This alpha version maps non-loop inputs to loop logic.
  • Stochasticity: Fails to account for real-world variance (passenger boarding delays, traffic lights) leading to bus bunching.
  • Crew Scheduling: Does not map driver shifts, union constraints, or mandatory meal breaks against the vehicle blocks.
